Background

Built From the Ground Up

Merit grew up in the Dallas–Fort Worth area, part of a family with roots running deep in Texas — the Deppe name traces back to German settlers who came to North Texas in the 1800s. That heritage instilled something durable: a work ethic that doesn't rely on shortcuts.

At Texas A&M, she earned a B.S. in Communication while simultaneously working as a pharmacy technician — not something most college students do. She wasn't padding a resume. She genuinely wanted to learn the language of healthcare from the inside.

Her four years in pharmacy gave her something no sales training program can replicate: thousands of real conversations with real patients about real fears — cost, side effects, insurance denials, needle anxiety. She navigated every one of them.

In Kappa Delta, she was consistently chosen to represent the chapter in competitive Panhellenic recruitment — a role built entirely on rapid rapport, authentic persuasion, and reading a room. Those are sales skills with a different name.
