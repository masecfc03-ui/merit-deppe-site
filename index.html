Why She's Different

Built From the Inside Out

Most entry-level pharma reps walk into a physician's office having never set foot in a clinical environment. Merit has spent four years inside one. She knows what a prior authorization feels like from the patient's side. She knows the exact moment a patient decides to trust you — and she knows how to get there.

The Deppe family has deep Texas roots — the name traces back to German settlers in North Texas in the 1800s. That history shows in Merit's approach: she's methodical, durable, and built for long games. Not a flash-in-the-pan. The kind of rep who's still managing that account five years later.

At Texas A&M's Kappa Delta — one of the largest Greek organizations on campus — she was consistently selected to represent the chapter in competitive Panhellenic recruitment. This isn't a social line item. Panhellenic recruitment is a structured, multi-day process where women are evaluated on their ability to build rapport instantly, hold conversations across dozens of one-on-one interactions, and convert. She participated in 90%+ of opportunities her senior year.

That is a sales skill. It has a different name in a sorority. The skill is identical.

Her Certificate in Health Communication from Texas A&M means she's not just fluent in pharmaceutical terminology — she's trained in message framing, health literacy, behavior change theory, and risk communication. That's what separates someone who can recite a drug's MOA from someone who can make a doctor change their prescribing habits.

She's not trying to break into healthcare. She's already in it. She's just moving to the other side of the table.
